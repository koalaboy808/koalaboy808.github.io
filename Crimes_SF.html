DogWatch:

A prediction of San Francisco Crime

[Spatial Analytics] [Python] [Machine Learning] [Folium] [Scikit-Learn]







San Francisco experiences no shortage of crime. A mixture of growing social and economic inequalities, housing disparity, and urban complexity all add to this unique problem. The city recently released a large open data set on Kaggle for the best to try their hand at predicting category of crime.

Given a granular set of locations, dates, and times of day, we were faced with the tricky task of creating valuable features at a more abstract level. We relied on visual exploratory analysis as our first method of extracting information. We then created new features and validated our scores using supervised learning techniques.

Heavily involved in entire process of exploring data, developing visualizations, creating features, and buidling classifiers.
